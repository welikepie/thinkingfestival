On the Friday between Thinking UX and UX Camp London, we’ve arranged an array of events to keep you busy. We’re organising tours of the most interesting agencies and startups, a ghostly walk around London, a trip to a museum and a coffee-tasting workshop.

Fringe Events

Ghost walks

London has a fantastic history that should be shared, though we'd like to share it with a twist. Luke Murphy, our resident ghost hunter, will be taking attendees around London and sharing with them the myths, tales and oddities that London has to offer. Luke will take everyone around London, crawling from pub to pub accross the city and exploring a range of tales and tidbits of history that have a scary undercurrent.

Coffee Blending

Zoe Rose is an information architect and content designer.

Zoe is interested in categories, descriptions, and metadata. She prefers to work in the messy space between natural and formal language.

Zoe works at gov.uk. She has also worked at the BBC, Pearson, and Cambridge University Press. When she tells people her first career was as a High school English teacher, they often don't believe her.

Museum Trip

Sjors Timmer is a freelance Interaction Designer interested in exploring and stretching the boundaries of design. He has worked for the Ministry of Justice, for agencies and start-ups.He is actively involved in the design community, where he co-organised UXcamp London and UXDO, a series of practical workshops for designers. He has given a number of presentations on a broad range of design subjects for diverse audiences of professionals, including a presentation on designing with time in mind at EuroIA and the design process of Rem Koolhaas at LondonIA. He has written for well-known blogs and has been featured in .Net magazine. He holds an MA in Digital Media from Goldsmiths, University of London.
